What is acetaminophen?
According to Mayo Clinic experts, one of the most common and effective option of pain relief medications is acetaminophen/paracetamol. Found in Tylenol, Actifed, Benadryl, other over-the-counter medications.

Pains and aches during pregnancy
During pregnancy, women may experience backaches, leg cramps, headaches, ligament pains. Most are thought to be the result of abundant hormonal changes. Acetaminophen/paracetamol is thought to be the safest pain relief medication for pregnant women all over the world.

The 2010’s: Decade of prenatal acetaminophen risk studies
Many scientific studies in the 2010’s suggest that prenatal acetaminophen is associated with children having asthma, lower IQ, poor male reproductive potential, autism, gross motor development and communication problems, ADHD, poorer attention and executive function, and behavioral problems in childhood.

2017: Risk of ADHD confirmed by a robust study (risk after 7 days)
Over 100,000 children born between 1999 and 2009, half were exposed to acetaminophen, which more than double the risk of developing ADHD. No risk under 7 days of consumption. Increased risk only periods of use longer than 7 days.

2019: Association of acetaminophen and asthma still under debate
There appears to be an association between maternal prenatal acetaminophen use and the risk of developing childhood wheezing and asthma. However the evidence is not sufficiently convincing to avoid the use of acetaminophen if clinically indicated.

Untreated fever and possible birth defects
Women who reported having a fever before or during early pregnancy were more likely to have a baby with a neural tube defect. However, women who reported having a fever before or during early pregnancy, but who were consuming folic acid every day, did not have this increased risk.
